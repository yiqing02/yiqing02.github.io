Theory - Making the Drake Equation Percise

The famous Drake equation is an order-of-magnitude probabilistic argument used to estimate the number of active, communicative extraterrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way Galaxy.

\[N = R_* \times f_p \times n_e \times f_l \times f_i \times f_c \times L\]

A common critique of SETI is its lack of robust theoretical foundations. Unlike other branches of physics, which rely on models to predict the prevalence of specific phenomena, SETI operates without such predictive frameworks. In physics, these models are tested experimentally, refining our understanding of fundamental principles, whereas SETI has yet to establish similar theoretical constraints. I hope to change this.

I am interested in the intersection of statistical mechanics, evolutionary dynamics, and game theory to merge to explain the structure formation of populations of living organisms in the universe.
